What Is It

A real store.
Real stakes.

Every ASCEND chapter operates a Spirit Store — a student-managed retail business that sells school merchandise, branded apparel, and everyday supplies to the school community.

Students don't play shopkeeper. They run a business. They manage stock and inventory, build and execute marketing campaigns, handle customer service, and produce monthly financial reports. They make decisions with real consequences.

The Spirit Store is ASCEND's most distinctive program — and for many students, it becomes the most formative experience of their school career.

▦
Inventory Management

Students track stock levels, manage purchase orders, and optimize inventory turnover — hands-on supply chain experience from day one.

◈
Financial Reporting

Monthly P&L statements, cash flow tracking, and budget management give students genuine financial literacy that goes far beyond classroom theory.

◉
Marketing & Promotion

Campaigns, social content, seasonal promotions, and visual merchandising — the marketing team drives awareness and footfall for every product launch.

◎
Customer Operations

Staff rotations, service standards, complaints handling, and satisfaction tracking build the customer-facing skills that employers consistently rank as hardest to find.

Student Roles

Every role.
Real responsibility.

The Spirit Store operates with a structure mirroring a real retail business. Each student takes on a defined role with clear accountability — because the most valuable learning happens when the stakes are genuine.

01
Store Manager

Oversees all operations, chairs weekly team meetings, liaises with the faculty advisor, and is accountable for the store's overall performance and culture.

  • General management
  • Team leadership
  • Strategic planning
  • Stakeholder communication
02
Finance Officer

Manages the store's finances — tracking revenue, controlling costs, producing monthly reports, and advising the team on budget decisions and pricing strategy.

  • P&L management
  • Cash flow tracking
  • Pricing strategy
  • Financial reporting
03
Marketing Lead

Owns the store's brand and promotional activity — from social campaigns to in-store visual merchandising — with a focus on driving awareness and conversion.

  • Campaign planning
  • Social media management
  • Visual merchandising
  • Brand strategy
04
Operations Lead

Responsible for inventory, supplier relationships, staffing rotations, and ensuring the physical store runs efficiently and to standard every operating day.

  • Inventory management
  • Supplier coordination
  • Staff scheduling
  • Quality control

Getting Started

Open your store
in three steps.

01
Join ASCEND

Apply as a member school. Once accepted, your chapter receives a Spirit Store onboarding kit — including setup guidelines, role descriptions, financial templates, and a sample product catalogue.

02
Build Your Team

Recruit students into the four core roles. ASCEND provides a role assignment framework and a structured induction curriculum to get your team confident and ready before opening day.

03
Open & Operate

Launch your store with your school community. From there, your student team runs it — with ongoing support from ASCEND and check-ins from your dedicated chapter advisor.
